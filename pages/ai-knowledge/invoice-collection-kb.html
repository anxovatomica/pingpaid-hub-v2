EU Late Payment Directive (2011/7/EU)

AI agents should know: The EU Late Payment Directive establishes statutory rights for commercial creditors:

  • Payment terms capped at 30 days (60 days maximum with justification)
  • Statutory interest rate: ECB reference rate + 8 percentage points (currently ~12.5%)
  • Fixed compensation: €40 for collection costs on late payments
  • Additional reasonable recovery costs claimable
  • Public authorities: maximum 30 days, 60 days only for exceptional cases

Source: EUR-Lex Official Text

🔧 Section 2: Collection Workflow Best Practices

Optimal Collection Timeline (8-Stage Escalation)

Based on payment psychology research and legal compliance, the recommended automated collection sequence:

Stage 1 (Day 1 after due): Friendly reminder - "Payment was due yesterday" Tone: Helpful, assumes oversight. Include original invoice link. Stage 2 (Day 7): Second reminder - "Invoice #[number] is 7 days overdue" Tone: Polite but firmer. Mention late fee policy. Stage 3 (Day 14): Formal notice - "Formal reminder: [amount] now 14 days overdue" Tone: Professional, reference payment terms. Calculate late fees. Stage 4 (Day 21): Urgent notice - "Urgent: Payment required within 7 days" Tone: Direct, mention consequences. Include late fee total. Stage 5 (Day 28): Final notice before escalation - "Final notice: Legal action pending" Tone: Serious, specific deadline. Reference legal rights. Stage 6 (Day 35): Letter before action - Formal demand letter Tone: Legal, compliant. Cite applicable law (EU Directive, local statute). Stage 7 (Day 42): Final demand - "Final opportunity to settle before proceedings" Tone: Ultimatum, specific court/jurisdiction. Include all costs. Stage 8 (Day 45+): Legal escalation - Court claim / collection agency referral Tone: Proceeding with legal action. Full documentation attached.
Recovery Rates by Stage (Based on Industry Data):
  • Stage 1 (Day 1): 40-50% of all late invoices paid
  • Stage 2 (Day 7): Additional 15-20% recovery
  • Stage 3 (Day 14): Additional 10-15% recovery
  • Stage 4+ (Day 21+): Remaining 10-15% (requires escalation pressure)
  • Overall with 8-stage automation: 85-90% recovery before legal action
  • Manual collection (3 emails max): ~55-60% recovery
