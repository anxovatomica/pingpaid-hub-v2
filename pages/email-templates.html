💰 Late Payment Reminder #1 (Friendly)

When: 7 days after due date

Subject: Invoice #[NUMBER] — Payment Reminder

Hi [Name],

I hope you're doing well! I wanted to follow up on invoice #[NUMBER] for $[AMOUNT], which was due on [DUE DATE].

I understand things get busy, so I wanted to send a friendly reminder. Please let me know if you need any clarification on the invoice or if there's an issue with payment.

You can view and pay the invoice here: [LINK]

Thanks so much,
[Your Name]

💰 Late Payment Reminder #2 (Firm)

When: 14 days after due date

Subject: URGENT: Invoice #[NUMBER] — Payment Overdue

Hi [Name],

This is a follow-up regarding invoice #[NUMBER] for $[AMOUNT], which was due on [DUE DATE] and is now [DAYS] days overdue.

Per our agreement, a late fee of $[LATE_FEE] has been applied. The new balance is $[NEW_TOTAL].

Please remit payment by [NEW_DUE_DATE] to avoid additional late fees and potential suspension of services.

If there are extenuating circumstances, please reach out so we can discuss.

Thank you,
[Your Name]

💡 Pro tip: Use our Late Fee Calculator to calculate the exact fee before sending this email.

💰 Late Payment Reminder #3 (Final Notice)

When: 30 days after due date

Subject: FINAL NOTICE: Invoice #[NUMBER] — Immediate Payment Required

Hi [Name],

Despite previous reminders, invoice #[NUMBER] for $[AMOUNT] remains unpaid [DAYS] days past the due date of [DUE DATE].

The current balance, including late fees, is $[NEW_TOTAL].

This is our final notice before escalating collection. Payment must be received within 7 days.

If payment is not received by [FINAL_DATE], we will:

1. Suspend all current and future work
2. Refer the matter to collections
3. Pursue legal remedies as outlined in our agreement

We would prefer to resolve this amicably. Please contact me immediately if you wish to discuss.

[Your Name]

📈 Rate Increase Notice

Subject: Updated Rate Schedule — Effective [DATE]

Please let me know if you have any questions.

Best,
[Your Name]

🚫 Scope Creep Response

Subject: Re: [Project] — Additional Request

Hi [Name],

Thanks for the additional request regarding [specific feature/task].

This falls outside the original scope we agreed on in [contract/email date]. The original scope includes:

[list original scope items]

The requested addition would require approximately [HOURS] additional hours at $[RATE]/hour, for a total of $[AMOUNT].

Would you like me to:

A) Proceed with the addition at the quoted rate
B) Add it to a Phase 2 scope for future work
C) Adjust the original scope to accommodate (may affect timeline)

Let me know how you'd like to proceed!

Best,
[Your Name]

💡 Pro tip:>/strong> Always present options, not problems. "Here are 3 ways we can handle this" beats "This isn't in scope."

❌ Turning Down a Project (Professional)

Subject: Re: [Project Name] — Availability Update

Hi [Name],

Thank you so much for considering me for [Project Name]. I really appreciate the opportunity.

After reviewing the project requirements and my current workload, I don't believe I can deliver the quality you deserve within your timeline. I'm currently committed to [number] projects through [date].

I don't want to take on work I can't execute excellently — that wouldn't be fair to you.

If your timeline is flexible, I'd be happy to revisit this in [month]. Or I can recommend [colleague name] who specializes in [specific area] and might be a great fit.

Thanks again for thinking of me. I hope we can work together on something in the future.

Best,
[Your Name]
