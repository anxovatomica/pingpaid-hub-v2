Rate Increase Letter Template

Raise your rates · Keep your clients · Updated June 2026

Raising rates is scary. Most freelancers never do it. Here's a template that works.

⚠️ Before you send this: Make sure you've delivered excellent work. Rate increases work best when clients are already happy. Send this after a successful project, not during a difficult one.

📧 The Rate Increase Letter

Subject: Updated Rates for [Year] — [Your Name]

Hi [Client Name],

I hope you're doing well! I've really enjoyed working with you on [Project/ongoing work].

I'm writing to let you know that my rates will be increasing as of [Date].

Current rate: $[Current Rate]/hour
New rate: $[New Rate]/hour

This increase reflects:

• [X] years of experience and specialized expertise in [your niche]
• The quality and results I've consistently delivered for your projects
• Market rates for [your service] in [current year]
• Additional skills/tools I've added since we started working together [e.g., "I completed my certification in X"]

I understand budgets are important. I'm happy to discuss how we can adjust the scope or timeline to work within your budget if needed.

The new rate will apply to all work starting [Date]. Any projects or hours logged before that date will be billed at the current rate.

I value our working relationship and look forward to continuing to deliver great results for you.

Please let me know if you have any questions.

Best regards,
[Your Name]

🎯 When to Raise Rates

  • After a big win: Client just praised your work? Perfect timing.
  • At contract renewal: Natural transition point, less awkward
  • After 6+ months: Long-term clients expect periodic increases
  • When you're overbooked: High demand = you can charge more
  • After new skills: Learned something valuable? Charge for it

📊 How Much to Increase

  • Standard increase: 10-15% annually
  • Significant increase: 20-25% if you've added major skills or the market has shifted
  • Premium increase: 50%+ if you were undercharging significantly
💡 Pro tip: Give 30-60 days notice. Never surprise a client with a same-day rate increase. And always offer to discuss scope adjustments if the new rate stretches their budget.
